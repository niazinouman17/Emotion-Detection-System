Emotion Types We Recognize

Happiness

A state of well-being characterized by positive emotions ranging from contentment to intense joy. Our system detects smiles, laughter, and positive vocal tones.

Sadness

Emotional pain associated with feelings of disadvantage, loss, or helplessness. Detected through facial expressions and vocal characteristics.

Anger

Strong feeling of annoyance, displeasure, or hostility. Identified through facial muscle tension, voice pitch, and speech patterns.

Surprise

Brief mental and physiological state due to unexpected events. Detected through eye widening and sudden vocal changes.

Fear

Response to perceived danger or threat. Recognized through facial expressions and physiological signals like increased heart rate.

Neutral

Baseline emotional state without strong positive or negative feelings. Detected through relaxed facial features and steady voice patterns.
